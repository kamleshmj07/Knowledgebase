<h2 id="all-about-purchasing-land">2. All About Purchasing Land</h2>
<h3 id="two-ways-to-buy-land">2.1 Two Ways to Buy Land</h3>
<ol>
<li><strong>Buy directly from a Farmer</strong> → Requires a Farmer Certificate</li>
<li><strong>Buy through an Agent</strong></li>
</ol>
<blockquote>
<p>📌 <strong>Note:</strong> States like <strong>TN (Tamil Nadu), AP (Andhra Pradesh), and MP (Madhya Pradesh)</strong> do <strong>not</strong> require a Farmer Certificate.</p>

</blockquote>

<h2 id="questions--suggestions-from-the-workshop">3. Questions &amp; Suggestions from the Workshop</h2>
<p>These were raised and discussed during the session:</p>
<ol>
<li>
<p><strong>Clear Title</strong> → Prefer land with only 1 or 2 names on the title. Easier to document and transfer.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>What is a “Tukda”?</strong> → A fragmented/irregular plot. Can be legally challenged in court — be cautious.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Legal consultation is very important.</strong> Do not skip this step.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>⭐ <strong>Can we do a survey BEFORE purchasing?</strong> → Yes! (See Section 5.1 for survey types.)</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Types of Land:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>N/A (Non-Agricultural)</li>
<li>Residential</li>
<li>Agricultural</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>30-Year Search Report &amp; Ownership Chain</strong> — The full paper trail should follow this sequence:</p>
<pre><code>30yr Search Report → Land Ownership History
→ Newspaper Notice → Agreement → Sale Deed
→ 7/12 → Aatha (?) → 8A → Mojni Nakasha (Map)
</code></pre>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Building a House on Agricultural Land:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Only <strong>4% or less</strong> of the land can be used to build a house.</li>
<li>Register <strong>Gharpatti</strong> at the local <strong>Gram Panchayat</strong>.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Rainwater Harvesting Feasibility:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Use <strong>government rainfall data websites</strong> to check if rainwater harvesting makes sense for your region before buying.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Talk to local people</strong> in and around the land before finalizing the purchase. They often know things that documents don’t show.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Kharekod / Hakkpatra (Share Certificate):</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Land distribution is decided by <strong>heredity</strong>.</li>
<li>Clarify who inherits and ensure mutual understanding among all heirs.</li>
<li>A <strong>Notary</strong> is required along with <strong>NOC from each heir</strong>.</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ol>

<h3 id="surveying-the-land">5.1 Surveying the Land</h3>
<blockquote>
<p>Survey is one of the <strong>most important</strong> first steps after (or even before) purchasing.</p>
</blockquote>
<p><strong>Order of surveys:</strong></p>
<ol>
<li><strong>Private Survey</strong> — Do this first</li>
<li><strong>Government Survey</strong> — Official demarcation</li>
<li><strong>Contour Mapping / Survey</strong> — ⭐ Very important, even for slight slopes. Helps in water flow planning.</li>
</ol>

<h3 id="water-survey-with-a-hydrologist">5.3 Water Survey with a Hydrologist</h3>
<ol>
<li>A <strong>meter</strong> is required for the motor used for water fetching.</li>
<li>Getting the meter connection:
<ul>
<li><strong>Legally:</strong> Takes <strong>2–3 months</strong></li>
<li><strong>Illegally:</strong> Takes <strong>15–20 days</strong> <em>(noted in workshop, not recommended)</em></li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><strong>3-Phase meter</strong> is needed if consumption is more than <strong>3,000 units</strong>.</li>
</ol>
<blockquote>
<p>🚩 <strong>Important:</strong> Dam authority <strong>permission is required</strong> if you plan to get water from a dam.</p>
</blockquote>
<hr>
<h3 id="earthwork">5.4 Earthwork</h3>
<ol>
<li>Shape the land to <strong>percolate water as much as possible</strong></li>
<li><strong>Don’t disturb too much earth</strong> — preserve the earth core</li>
<li>Planting ratio: <strong>80% perennial</strong> crops &amp; <strong>20% annual</strong> crops <em>(adjust as per need)</em></li>
</ol>
<hr>
<h3 id="plantation-strategy">5.5 Plantation Strategy</h3>
<p><strong>Getting Started:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Polyhouse for selling vegetables</li>
<li>Try <strong>by-products</strong> (e.g., sun-drying produce)</li>
<li>Start with <strong>small targets</strong> — plant what you can manage</li>
<li>Explore <strong>heirloom seeds</strong> <em>(research needed)</em></li>
<li>For each tree ask: What space does it need? What is it giving back? Is its need here fulfilled?</li>
<li><strong>Draw a diagram</strong> of your land and map the <strong>sun’s location and path</strong> across it</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Tree Planning:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Plan <strong>shade trees</strong> where needed</li>
<li>Plan <strong>windbreaker trees</strong> along the boundary</li>
<li>Find trees that are <strong>evergreen in your specific region</strong></li>
</ul>

<h3 id="percolation--water-recharge-strategy">5.6 Percolation &amp; Water Recharge Strategy</h3>
<ul>
<li>Create <strong>percolation tanks, blocks, and trenching</strong> to enable <strong>groundwater recharge</strong>
<ul>
<li>This process takes <strong>7–10 years</strong> to fully show results — be patient</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><strong>Neem water with oil</strong> works effectively as a <strong>natural pest repellent</strong></li>
<li>If pests are <strong>not eating your produce</strong>, that’s a <strong>good sign</strong> of a healthy ecosystem</li>
<li><strong>Scout the land thoroughly</strong> at different times of day/season before finalizing design decisions</li>
</ul>
<hr>
<h2 id="workarounds-—-buying-land-without-a-farmer-certificate">6. Workarounds — Buying Land Without a Farmer Certificate</h2>
<blockquote>
<p>⚖️ <em>These are options discussed at the workshop. Always verify legality with a qualified legal advisor before proceeding. These are workarounds, not standard procedures.</em></p>
</blockquote>
<ol>
<li><strong>Application to District Collector</strong> → Submit an application for purchasing land without a farmer/land certificate</li>
<li><strong>Gift Deed</strong> → A legal route where land is gifted from the seller (some payment may still be involved)</li>
<li><strong>Transfer Route</strong> → Buy a small piece of land elsewhere (in a state/area where it is not required), get registered as a land owner, then buy the desired land in Maharashtra after the transfer</li>
<li>✅ <strong>Always consult a legal advisor</strong> before using any of the above approaches</li>
</ol>
